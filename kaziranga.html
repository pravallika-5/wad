Discover Kaziranga

Kaziranga National Park, located in Assam, India, is renowned for its population of the Indian one-horned rhinoceros, along with diverse wildlife and bird species, attracting visitors from around the globe.

Spanning approximately 430 square kilometers, Kaziranga features floodplain grasslands, wetlands, and tropical forests. It is home to various wildlife, including tigers, elephants, and water buffalo, making it a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a sanctuary for rare species.

Top Attractions

Kohora Range

★

The central range known for its dense rhino population and rich biodiversity.

Bagori Range

Western range famous for elephant and rhino sightings, offering breathtaking views of the landscape.

Agoratoli Range

Eastern range known for birdwatching and scenic beauty, ideal for photography enthusiasts.

Travel Tips

Here are some essential tips for your visit:

  • November to April - Peak Season
    • Comfortable temperatures and dry season, making it easier to spot animals.
    • Popular for safaris, birdwatching, and nature photography.
  • April to May - Best for Rhino Sightings
    • Warmer weather increases visibility near water bodies.
    • Ideal for witnessing wildlife up close.
