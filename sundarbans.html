Discover Sundarbans

The Sundarbans, located in the delta region of West Bengal, India, is famed for its dense mangrove forests and is the largest habitat of the Bengal tiger.

Covering approximately 10,000 square kilometers, the Sundarbans are home to diverse wildlife including saltwater crocodiles, spotted deer, and a vast range of bird species, making it a UNESCO World Heritage Site and an important ecological zone.

Top Attractions

Sajnekhali Wildlife Sanctuary

★

Known for its tiger reserve, mangrove forests, and crocodile conservation area.

Dobanki Watch Tower

Offers panoramic views of the forest, and chances to spot wildlife including the Bengal tiger.

Sundarban Tiger Camp

A prime spot for safaris, showcasing the beauty and rich biodiversity of the Sundarbans.

Travel Tips

Plan your trip to the Sundarbans with these essential tips:

  • November to February - Ideal for Wildlife
    • Cool temperatures and dry season make it easier to explore and spot wildlife.
    • Perfect for boat safaris and birdwatching.
  • March to May - Warmer, Great for Photography
    • Higher temperatures make sightings of tigers and other animals more likely near water sources.
    • Ideal for adventure and photography enthusiasts.
