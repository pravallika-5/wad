Discover Andaman and Nicobar Islands

"The Andaman and Nicobar Islands, a pristine paradise of turquoise waters and lush rainforests, beckon travelers to explore its tropical beauty, rich marine life, and vibrant indigenous cultures.".


Geographical Location and Surroundings: Located in the Bay of Bengal, the Andaman and Nicobar Islands form an archipelago that lies around 1,200 km southeast of mainland India. This Union Territory consists of over 500 islands, of which only a few are open to tourists. The Andaman Islands are closer to Myanmar, while the Nicobar Islands are closer to Indonesia. The capital, Port Blair, serves as the main entry point and is situated on South Andaman Island. The islands are surrounded by coral reefs, diverse marine life, and dense tropical forests, with famous spots like Radhanagar Beach on Havelock Island and the Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park. The islands are known for their ecological diversity and protected tribal territories, adding to their unique natural appeal.

Best Time to Visit Andaman and Nicobar Islands

  • Best Time to Visit
    • Winter (October to February) is the best time, with mild, pleasant weather ranging from 20°C to 30°C, ideal for sightseeing and beach activities.
    • Summer (March to May) is warm, great for water sports, but humidity can be high.
    • Monsoon (June to September) has heavy rains, limiting outdoor activities but offering off-season discounts for budget travelers.
  • Special Attractions
    • Explore Radhanagar Beach and Elephant Beach, known for pristine sand and crystal-clear waters.
    • Visit Cellular Jail in Port Blair for historical insights.
    • Enjoy birdwatching and sunsets at Chidiya Tapu.
  • Adventures to Try
    • Snorkeling and scuba diving to explore vibrant coral reefs and marine life.
    • Parasailing, surfing, and kayaking are popular during winter and early summer for ideal water visibility.
    • Trekking and island hopping offer adventure and scenic exploration.
  • Food Specialties
    • Try local seafood dishes like crab curry, grilled fish, and coconut prawn curry.
    • Enjoy tropical fruits and Indian-Southeast Asian fusion dishes.
    • Don't miss out on local sweets such as banana fritters and coconut-based desserts.
