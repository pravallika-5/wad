Top 7 Places to Visit in Coorg

    Abbey Falls

    Abbey Falls: Famous for its scenic waterfalls surrounded by lush coffee plantations, offering a mesmerizing view, especially during the monsoon season.

    Abbey Falls is a beautiful waterfall nestled within the dense coffee plantations of Coorg, Karnataka. The cascading water falls from a height of about 70 feet, creating a stunning sight amid lush greenery. It’s particularly captivating during the monsoon season when the water flow is at its peak. A hanging bridge near the falls allows visitors to enjoy panoramic views and take memorable photos.

    Raja’s Seat

    Raja’s Seat: Known for its stunning panoramic views of the valley and picturesque gardens, historically enjoyed by Coorg’s royalty.

    Raja’s Seat, meaning “Seat of the Kings,” is a charming garden and viewpoint that offers mesmerizing views of the valley and rolling hills of Coorg. Known for its stunning sunsets, the garden is decorated with seasonal flowers, fountains, and lush greenery. Historically, this was a favored spot for Coorg’s kings to relax and admire the landscape.

    Dubare Elephant Camp

    Dubare Elephant Camp: Renowned for its interactive experiences with elephants, allowing visitors to feed, bathe, and learn about these majestic animals.

    Dubare Elephant Camp is a unique and interactive experience that lets visitors get up close with elephants. Located along the banks of the River Cauvery, this camp offers activities like elephant bathing, feeding, and rides, along with insights into the lives of these majestic animals. It’s an ideal spot for wildlife enthusiasts and families to learn about elephant conservation.

    Talacauvery

    Talacauvery: Sacred as the origin point of the River Cauvery, offering spiritual ambiance and breathtaking views from Brahmagiri Hill.

    Talacauvery is the sacred birthplace of the River Cauvery, one of South India’s most revered rivers. Situated on Brahmagiri Hill, this pilgrimage site has a small temple and a tank that marks the origin of the river. Visitors often come to pray and immerse themselves in the peaceful atmosphere. The surrounding hills offer breathtaking panoramic views, adding to the spiritual ambiance.

    Golden Temple (Namdroling Monastery)

    Golden Temple (Namdroling Monastery): Known for its vibrant Tibetan architecture, golden statues, and peaceful setting, attracting visitors to explore Buddhist culture.

    The Namdroling Monastery, popularly known as the Golden Temple, is a Tibetan monastery located in Bylakuppe near Coorg. The monastery houses thousands of monks and showcases stunning Tibetan architecture with golden statues, vibrant murals, and intricate paintings. It’s a serene place where visitors can witness Tibetan Buddhist rituals and soak in the peaceful atmosphere.

    Iruppu Falls

    Iruppu Falls: Revered for its natural beauty and mythological significance, surrounded by dense forest and ideal for trekking.

    Iruppu Falls is a magnificent waterfall located within the Brahmagiri Hills in Coorg. The water cascades down from a height of around 170 feet, creating a soothing sound and a refreshing mist. It’s a popular spot for trekkers and nature lovers, as well as a sacred place linked to Hindu mythology. The path leading to the falls offers scenic forest views, adding to the experience.

    Nisargadhama

    Nisargadhama: A serene river island perfect for nature lovers, featuring bamboo groves, elephant rides, and a hanging bridge amidst tranquil scenery.

    Nisargadhama is a tranquil island formed by the River Cauvery, ideal for nature lovers and families. Known for its bamboo groves, sandalwood, and teak trees, it offers activities like boating, elephant rides, and picnicking. A hanging bridge connects the island, adding a fun element to the visit. Nisargadhama’s peaceful atmosphere and rich biodiversity make it a must-visit spot in Coorg.
