Discover Manali

"Manali, a charming hill station nestled in the majestic Himalayas, offers travelers a perfect blend of scenic beauty, adventure, and tranquility, making it a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

Manali is located in the northern state of Himachal Pradesh, India, at an altitude of about 2,050 meters (6,726 feet) above sea level. Situated in the Kullu Valley, it lies along the Beas River and is surrounded by snow-capped peaks of the Pir Panjal mountain range. Manali is approximately 270 km from the state capital, Shimla, and about 540 km from Delhi. The town is enveloped by lush forests of pine, cedar, and oak, and its proximity to the Rohtang Pass makes it a popular gateway to Ladakh. The surrounding region offers an enchanting mix of alpine meadows, rivers, and dramatic landscapes, making it a hub for trekkers and adventurers.

Travel Tips

For the best travel experience, explore the resources below:

  • Best Time to Visit
    • Winter (December to February)
      • Manali experiences snowfall and chilly weather, with temperatures ranging from -1°C to 10°C, perfect for snow sports and a cozy mountain escape.
      • Ideal for tourists who enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and snow trekking at Rohtang Pass.
      • This is also the peak season, attracting visitors from across the world to enjoy winter activities.
    • Summer (March to June)
      • Temperatures range from 10°C to 25°C, making it the most pleasant time for sightseeing, trekking, and adventure sports like paragliding.
      • The lush green landscapes and blooming flowers offer a picturesque environment, especially around Solang Valley.
      • This is the best time for outdoor activities and exploring the beauty of Manali without the cold of winter.
    • Monsoon (July to September)
      • Manali experiences heavy rainfall, with temperatures between 15°C to 20°C, which can make outdoor activities difficult.
      • However, the monsoon brings lush greenery, making it a good time for those looking for a quiet retreat.
      • Not recommended for trekking or adventure sports due to slippery conditions and roadblocks caused by landslides.
  • Special Attractions
    • Rohtang Pass - Famous for its snow-capped mountains and adventure sports, including skiing, snowboarding, and tobogganing, especially during winter.
    • Hadimba Temple - A unique, ancient temple set amidst tall cedar trees, offering a spiritual experience and stunning architecture.
    • Solang Valley - Known for its adventure activities like paragliding, zorbing, and skiing, it offers a panoramic view of the surrounding peaks.
    • Manu Temple - Dedicated to the sage Manu, this temple is set in the heart of Old Manali and provides a peaceful atmosphere with magnificent views.
  • Adventures to Do
    • Skiing and Snowboarding - Best during the winter months (December to February), particularly at Rohtang Pass, which turns into a winter wonderland.
    • Paragliding - Enjoy the thrill of flying in Solang Valley, best during summer when the weather is ideal for aerial views of the mountains and valleys.
    • Trekking - The trekking season begins in summer and stretches into autumn. Popular treks include the Beas Kund Trek, Bhrigu Lake Trek, and Hampta Pass Trek.
    • Mountain Biking - Explore the rugged terrains around Manali during the summer months, where you can bike through scenic mountain trails.
