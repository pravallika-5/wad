Top 7 Places to Visit in Jim Corbett

    Jim Corbett National Park

    Jim Corbett National Park:India’s oldest national park, known for its rich biodiversity and famous tiger sightings, offering thrilling safaris through diverse landscapes.

    Located in Uttarakhand, Jim Corbett National Park is India’s first national park, famous for its diverse wildlife, especially the Bengal tiger. The park offers thrilling jeep safaris, bird watching, and a chance to explore a range of ecosystems from dense forests to grasslands. It's a top destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts alike.

    Corbett Waterfall

    Corbett Waterfall: A serene waterfall surrounded by dense forests, providing a peaceful escape and a picturesque picnic spot.

    Nestled amidst the picturesque landscapes of Corbett, this waterfall is a serene spot for visitors to unwind. A short trek through the forest leads you to the cascading waters, surrounded by lush greenery, making it an ideal place for a peaceful retreat.

    Garjiya Devi Temple

    Garjiya Devi Temple: A revered hilltop temple dedicated to Goddess Garjiya, attracting devotees and offering beautiful views of the Kosi River below.

    Situated on a hillock near the Kosi River, Garjiya Devi Temple is a significant pilgrimage site dedicated to Goddess Garjiya. The temple’s location offers panoramic views of the river and surrounding forest, making it a serene and spiritual destination.

    Dhikala Zone

    Dhikala Zone:The most popular safari zone in Jim Corbett, known for its panoramic landscapes, wildlife sightings, and breathtaking views of the Ramganga River.

    The Dhikala Zone of Jim Corbett National Park is known for its stunning landscapes and high probability of wildlife sightings, especially tigers, elephants, and a variety of birds. This area, located along the Ramganga River, provides some of the best views in the park and is a must-visit for safari lovers.

    Sitabani Temple

    Sitabani Temple: An ancient temple set in the tranquil Sitabani forest area, associated with Hindu mythology and offering a peaceful ambiance.

    Located in the tranquil Sitabani Forest area, Sitabani Temple is an ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Rama and Goddess Sita. It’s believed to be the site where Sita gave birth to her sons, Lava and Kusha. The temple is a peaceful spot for both pilgrims and nature lovers.

    Kosi River

    Kosi River:A scenic river flowing along Jim Corbett National Park, popular for river rafting, bird watching, and scenic picnics.

    The Kosi River flows through the Jim Corbett National Park, offering a scenic backdrop for the park’s natural beauty. It is also popular for fishing, boating, and rafting, and the area around the river is a great place for birdwatching, with several species spotted along its banks.

    Kalagarh Dam

    Kalagarh Dam:A large dam on the Ramganga River, known for its scenic surroundings and as a bird-watching spot, especially in the winter season.

    Built on the Ramganga River, the Kalagarh Dam is an essential part of the region's irrigation system. The area around the dam is popular for its scenic beauty and wildlife, making it a great spot for photography, bird watching, and nature walks.
