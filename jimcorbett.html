Discover Jim Corbett

Jim Corbett National Park, situated in Uttarakhand, India, is renowned for its Bengal tiger population and beautiful landscapes, attracting wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers worldwide.

Covering approximately 520 square kilometers, Jim Corbett features diverse topography of hills, riverine belts, marshy depressions, grasslands, and a large lake. It is home to various species, including tigers, elephants, leopards, and over 600 bird species.

Top Attractions

Dhikala Zone

★

A prime area for tiger sightings, known for its stunning valley views and rich wildlife.

Bijrani Zone

Another popular safari zone known for its diverse flora and fauna, providing high chances of wildlife sightings.

Garjia Temple

A sacred temple located on a large rock in the Kosi River, attracting visitors for its scenic beauty and spirituality.

Travel Tips

Plan your trip with these helpful travel tips:

  • November to February - Ideal for Safari
    • Pleasant daytime temperatures and cool evenings, ideal for animal sightings.
    • Popular period for tiger spotting and nature walks.
  • April to June - High Tiger Sightings
    • High temperatures but excellent visibility of tigers near water sources.
    • Best for adventure and wildlife photography.
