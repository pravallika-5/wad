Top 7 Places to Visit in Varkala

    Varkala Beach

    Varkala Beach: Known for its golden sands, striking cliffs, and stunning views of the Arabian Sea.

    Varkala Beach, known for its golden sands and serene waters, is a popular spot for both relaxation and water activities. This beautiful beach is surrounded by dramatic cliffs, creating a unique coastal landscape. It's a favorite among both locals and tourists for sunbathing, swimming, and enjoying the peaceful atmosphere. The beach also has various cafes and shops offering local goods and fresh seafood, making it a perfect place to unwind and enjoy the sunset.

    Papanasam Beach

    Papanasam Beach: Famous for its sacred significance, believed to cleanse sins and offer spiritual rejuvenation to visitors.

    Papanasam Beach, located near Varkala, holds deep spiritual significance and is believed to cleanse one’s sins according to Hindu traditions. The beach is renowned for its peaceful vibe and natural beauty, making it an ideal place for reflection and meditation. Pilgrims visit Papanasam for its sacred water, which is believed to purify the soul. The temple near the beach adds to its spiritual charm, and visitors can often see people performing rituals and taking dips in the holy waters.

    anardhana

    Anardhana: Known for its vibrant atmosphere and serene landscapes, perfect for a peaceful retreat.

    Anardhana is a small, peaceful retreat that offers a tranquil atmosphere amidst nature. Located near Varkala, it is a perfect spot for those seeking relaxation and a break from the hustle and bustle of the more crowded beaches. Surrounded by lush greenery, this place is ideal for nature walks, meditation, or just enjoying the quiet beauty of the area. It's a lesser-known gem for those looking to escape the tourist crowds and enjoy a calm, scenic environment.

    Varkala Cliff

    Varkala Cliff: Famous for its dramatic red cliffs overlooking the Arabian Sea, offering stunning panoramic views and vibrant cafés.

    The Varkala Cliff is one of the most famous natural attractions in Varkala, offering stunning views of the Arabian Sea. The cliff is lined with shops, cafés, and restaurants, making it a popular hangout spot for travelers. The views from the top of the cliff, especially during sunset, are breathtaking, and the area provides great opportunities for photography. The cliff also overlooks Varkala Beach, offering a bird’s-eye view of the coastline and the waves crashing below.

    Kappil Lake

    Kappil Lake: A serene lake perfect for boating, bordered by coconut groves and offering beautiful views of the backwaters meeting the sea.

    Kappil Lake is a serene and picturesque spot located near the Varkala Beach. The lake is surrounded by coconut groves and is an excellent place for a peaceful boat ride. Visitors can take a leisurely boat ride along the lake, enjoying the tranquility and natural beauty. The calm waters of the lake meeting the sea at the distant horizon create a magical, peaceful experience. It is an ideal place to unwind and enjoy nature, especially for those seeking a quiet getaway.

    Sivagiri Mutt

    Sivagiri Mutt: A spiritual center devoted to the teachings of Sree Narayana Guru, drawing pilgrims and visitors alike for meditation and peace.

    Sivagiri Mutt is an important pilgrimage site for followers of Sree Narayana Guru, one of the most revered saints and social reformers of Kerala. Located atop a hill, the mutt is a peaceful and spiritual retreat for those seeking meditation and reflection. It is also a place where people come to learn about the teachings of Sree Narayana Guru, which emphasize social equality, education, and spiritual awakening. The serene surroundings of Sivagiri Mutt make it a perfect place for peace and introspection.

    Anjengo Fort

    Anjengo Fort: A historic fort with colonial-era ruins, offering scenic views and insight into Kerala's colonial past.

    Anjengo Fort, located near Varkala, is a historic site dating back to the 17th century. Built by the British East India Company, the fort played a significant role in the colonial history of the region. Though now in ruins, the fort’s remnants offer insight into Kerala’s colonial past. Surrounded by lush greenery and offering panoramic views of the nearby coastline, Anjengo Fort is a peaceful and educational spot for history enthusiasts and visitors interested in exploring the area’s past.
