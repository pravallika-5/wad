Discover Goa

A kaleidoscopic blend of Indian and Portuguese cultures, sweetened with sun, sea, sand, seafood, susegad and spirituality, Goa is India's pocket-sized paradise.

Goa is a small state located on the western coast of India, bordered by the Arabian Sea. It lies between the states of Maharashtra to the north and Karnataka to the south and east. Known for its stunning coastline, Goa spans approximately 101 kilometers (63 miles) along the Arabian Sea and is about 3,700 square kilometers (1,429 square miles) in area, making it one of the smallest states in India. The state capital, Panaji (also known as Panjim), is situated along the Mandovi River, while Vasco da Gama is its largest city. Goa's central location along India's Konkan Coast makes it a popular and accessible destination for both Indian and international tourists.

Travel Tips

For the best travel experience, explore the resources below:

  • November to February - Ideal Season
    • The weather is mild and pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 20°C to 28°C. This season is perfect for enjoying the beaches, sightseeing, and outdoor activities without the extreme heat.
    • Food< Specialties: This season is great for savoring fresh seafood like Goan prawn curry, fish recheado (spicy, stuffed fish), and bebinca, a layered Goan dessert.
    • Activities: Visitors can experience vibrant local festivals, such as the Sunburn Festival in December. It's also an ideal time for water sports, including scuba diving, parasailing, and jet skiing. Shopping in flea markets and attending beach parties are popular as well.
  • March to May - Off-Peak Season
    • Reason: Temperatures rise, reaching between 25°C and 35°C, and humidity is higher. This season is less crowded, offering a quieter experience and often more affordable rates on accommodations and travel.
    • Food Specialties: Enjoy seasonal treats like feni, a local cashew-based spirit, and cashew nuts, which are harvested during this period.
    • Activities: While it’s warm, early mornings and evenings are good for beach visits and exploring historical landmarks like forts. This period also offers a more local vibe, with fewer tourists around.
  • June to October - Monsoon Season
    • Reason: Goa experiences heavy rains, making the landscape lush and green, though beach activities are limited. This season is suitable for those looking to explore Goa’s natural beauty and monsoon vibes rather than its beaches.
    • Food Specialties: Monsoon brings seasonal Goan dishes like patoleo (a sweet made from rice, coconut, and jaggery, steamed in turmeric leaves) and spicy fish curries perfect for rainy weather.
    • Activities: Visitors can trek to scenic waterfalls like Dudhsagar Falls, enjoy eco-tourism, and experience Goa's peaceful, verdant landscape..
