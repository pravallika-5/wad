Discover Darjeeling

"Darjeeling, often referred to as the 'Queen of the Hills,' is a picturesque hill station in West Bengal, famous for its tea gardens, breathtaking views of the Kanchenjunga mountain range, and its charming colonial architecture."

Darjeeling is located in the northeastern part of India, nestled in the Himalayas at an altitude of about 2,000 meters (6,500 feet) above sea level. It is situated in the state of West Bengal, near the borders of Nepal and Bhutan, and is surrounded by lush green hills and tea plantations. The town offers a panoramic view of the mighty Kanchenjunga, the third-highest mountain in the world. Darjeeling is part of the Darjeeling district, and it lies at the foothills of the Eastern Himalayas. The town is also known for its proximity to the Sikkim state, providing travelers with a blend of different cultural influences, including Tibetan, Nepali, and Bengali. Its cool climate, rich biodiversity, and colonial-era charm make it a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of the plains.

Travel Tips

For the best travel experience, explore the resources below:

  • Best Time to Visit Darjeeling
    • Summer (March to June)
      • The weather is pleasant with temperatures ranging from 10°C to 25°C, making it ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities.
      • Summer is the most popular time to visit, as the town remains cool and offers clear views of the Kanchenjunga mountain range.
      • Perfect for exploring tea estates, visiting **Tiger Hill** for sunrise views, and taking a ride on the **Darjeeling Himalayan Railway** (Toy Train), a UNESCO World Heritage site.
    • Monsoon (July to September)
      • During the monsoon, Darjeeling experiences heavy rainfall, with temperatures between 12°C to 22°C. This is the least ideal time to visit due to the risk of landslides and roadblocks.
      • The lush greenery is in full bloom, and though the weather is wet, the landscape is vibrant and scenic. It is a good time for nature lovers, but caution is needed due to the weather conditions.
      • Monsoon also marks the off-season, so tourists can enjoy a quieter experience but should be prepared for unpredictable weather.
    • Autumn (October to November)
      • Autumn offers crisp and clear weather with temperatures ranging from 10°C to 20°C, making it a perfect time for sightseeing and trekking.
      • The skies are clear, and views of the Kanchenjunga peak are breathtaking. This is an ideal time for outdoor activities such as trekking in nearby **Singalila National Park**.
      • Autumn is also a great time to explore Darjeeling’s tea estates, as the tea harvest season begins during this period.
    • Winter (December to February)
      • Winter in Darjeeling is cold, with temperatures ranging from 2°C to 10°C, and can dip even lower at night, especially in higher altitudes.
      • Though the weather is chilly, it’s a great time for winter photography and enjoying the peaceful, snow-capped surroundings, especially in areas like **Lover’s Meet**.
      • Winter is ideal for snow sports and adventure activities in nearby areas, and the low season offers fewer crowds for a more tranquil experience.
  • Special Attractions to Watch
    • Tiger Hill - Famous for its sunrise view over the Kanchenjunga range, especially stunning in the summer and autumn months.
    • Darjeeling Himalayan Railway - A UNESCO World Heritage Site, best enjoyed during the clear months of summer and autumn when the weather is perfect for train rides.
    • Peace Pagoda - A beautiful Buddhist stupa offering panoramic views of the town and surrounding mountains, ideal for a peaceful retreat in all seasons.
    • Tea Gardens - The tea gardens are best explored in summer and autumn when the tea harvest season is at its peak, offering a unique opportunity to experience the production of Darjeeling tea.
  • Adventures to Do
    • Trekking - Darjeeling offers several trekking routes, with the most famous being the **Singalila Ridge Trek**, which provides scenic views of the Kanchenjunga range. Best done in summer or autumn.
    • Rock Climbing - The rugged landscape makes Darjeeling a perfect spot for rock climbing, available throughout the year but best enjoyed in the cooler months of autumn and winter.
    • Mountain Biking - For adventure enthusiasts, biking through the hills and tea gardens offers an exciting challenge, ideal during the summer months when the weather is pleasant.
    • Paragliding - Available in certain regions near Darjeeling, this adventure sport can be best enjoyed during the clearer months of spring and autumn.
  • Special Mentions
    • The **Bengal Safari Park**, located near Darjeeling, is a unique wildlife sanctuary that offers a chance to observe the region's rich biodiversity, best visited in autumn when the weather is ideal for nature walks.
    • The **Darjeeling Flower Show** in spring (March) is an exciting event where visitors can witness the town’s vibrant floral displays and participate in horticultural activities.
