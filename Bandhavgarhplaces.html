Top 7 Places to Visit in Bandhavgarh

    Bandhavgarh National Park

    Bandhavgarh National Park:A renowned wildlife sanctuary known for its large tiger population and diverse wildlife, ideal for safaris.

    A world-renowned wildlife sanctuary, Bandhavgarh is celebrated for its dense tiger population and incredible diversity of flora and fauna. This park is a prime destination for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers, offering thrilling safari experiences in the heart of nature.

    Bandhavgarh Fort

    Bandhavgarh Fort:A historic fort located on a hill, offering panoramic views of the surrounding park and valleys.

    Perched atop a hill within the park, Bandhavgarh Fort is a historic structure steeped in legend and lore. From here, visitors can enjoy sweeping views of the park’s lush valleys and forests, making it an unforgettable vantage point.

    Taala Zone

    Taala Zone:A popular safari zone in the park, known for frequent tiger sightings and rich biodiversity.

    This popular safari zone within the park is known for frequent tiger sightings, dense forests, and abundant wildlife. The Taala Zone's vibrant biodiversity offers visitors an immersive experience in one of the most wildlife-rich areas of the park.

    Bamni Dadar

    Bamni Dadar:A scenic spot known as the "Sunset Point," offering breathtaking views of the park and its wildlife.

    Also known as "Sunset Point," Bamni Dadar is a breathtaking viewpoint renowned for its stunning views at dusk. Visitors can take in expansive panoramas of the park and watch animals roaming freely in their natural habitat as the sun sets.

    Chakradhara

    Chakradhara:A peaceful location within the park, popular for its tranquil atmosphere and wildlife sightings, especially tigers.

    Known for its calm and serene ambiance, Chakradhara is a beautiful, open space within the park. It’s especially popular for those looking to encounter tigers in a tranquil setting, as well as other wildlife in a peaceful atmosphere.

    Shesh Shaiya

    Shesh Shaiya: A revered statue of Lord Vishnu reclining on the serpent Shesha, located near the Bandhavgarh Fort.

    This sacred statue depicts Lord Vishnu reclining on the cosmic serpent, Shesha, near the Bandhavgarh Fort. Shesh Shaiya is a place of reverence and history, attracting visitors with its spiritual significance and scenic surroundings.

    Magadhi Zone

    Chapora Fort:A safari zone famous for its picturesque landscapes, wildlife, and opportunities to spot tigers, leopards, and other animals.

    Known for its scenic beauty, the Magadhi Zone offers a safari experience rich with landscapes, forests, and a variety of animals including tigers and leopards. This zone provides ample opportunities for wildlife enthusiasts to experience the park’s wilderness.
