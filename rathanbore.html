Discover Ranthambore

Ranthambore is a renowned wildlife sanctuary in Rajasthan, India, famous for its majestic Bengal tigers and historic Ranthambore Fort, drawing nature enthusiasts and wildlife photographers from around the world.

Situated in the Sawai Madhopur district of southeastern Rajasthan, Ranthambore National Park covers an area of approximately 1,334 square kilometers. The park lies at the junction of the Aravalli and Vindhya mountain ranges, creating a unique topography of rugged hills, dense forests, and grassy meadows. Bordered by the Chambal and Banas Rivers, Ranthambore's varied landscape is home to diverse wildlife, including leopards, sloth bears, and a range of bird species. The historic Ranthambore Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage site, overlooks the park, adding a fascinating cultural dimension to the natural beauty of the region.

Travel Tips

For the best travel experience, explore the resources below:

  • October to March - Ideal for Wildlife Sightings
    • Pleasant, with daytime temperatures between 10°C to 25°C (50°F to 77°F), Cool mornings and evenings, making it ideal for safaris
    • The dry season, with minimal rainfall, making it easier to spot wildlife
    • Activities: Morning and afternoon safaris for prime wildlife sightings, Fort trekking to explore the historic Ranthambore Fort, Nature trails and guided birdwatching tours.
  • April to July - Best for Tiger Sightings
    • Reason: Dry climate but offers high chances of spotting tigers by water bodies.
    • Activities: Morning and afternoon safaris, Birdwatching, and exploring the fort.
    • Early morning safaris to catch tigers cooling off by water holes
    • September to October - Off-Season with Lush Surroundings
      • Reason: Moderate to heavy rainfall, making the park green and refreshing, Temperatures range between 25°C to 35°C (77°F to 95°F)
      • Activities: Exploring Ranthambore’s outskirts for smaller reserves and eco-tourism
      • Fort and cultural tours, showcasing local history and heritage
      • Photography of the monsoon-drenched greenery and landscapes
