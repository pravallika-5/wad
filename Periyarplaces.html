Top 7 Places to Visit in Periyar

    Calangute Beach

    Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary:A popular sanctuary known for its elephants, tigers, and rich biodiversity, offering boat safaris on Periyar Lake.

    A world-renowned wildlife sanctuary, Bandhavgarh is celebrated for its dense tiger population and incredible diversity of flora and fauna. This park is a prime destination for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers, offering thrilling safari experiences in the heart of nature.

    Periyar Lake

    Periyar Lake:A serene, man-made lake surrounded by lush forests, ideal for boat rides and wildlife viewing.

    Perched atop a hill within the park, Bandhavgarh Fort is a historic structure steeped in legend and lore. From here, visitors can enjoy sweeping views of the park’s lush valleys and forests, making it an unforgettable vantage point.

    Spice Plantations

    Spice Plantations:Tour aromatic spice plantations that offer insight into the cultivation of cardamom, pepper, and other spices.

    This popular safari zone within the park is known for frequent tiger sightings, dense forests, and abundant wildlife. The Taala Zone's vibrant biodiversity offers visitors an immersive experience in one of the most wildlife-rich areas of the park.

    Thekkady

    Thekkady: A vibrant town near the sanctuary, known for its natural beauty and various adventure activities like bamboo rafting and trekking.

    Also known as "Sunset Point," Bamni Dadar is a breathtaking viewpoint renowned for its stunning views at dusk. Visitors can take in expansive panoramas of the park and watch animals roaming freely in their natural habitat as the sun sets.

    Chellarkovil Viewpoint

    Chellarkovil Viewpoint:A picturesque spot offering stunning views of cascading waterfalls and the green valleys below.

    Known for its calm and serene ambiance, Chakradhara is a beautiful, open space within the park. It’s especially popular for those looking to encounter tigers in a tranquil setting, as well as other wildlife in a peaceful atmosphere

    Mullaperiyar Dam

    Palolem Beach:A historic dam surrounded by scenic landscapes, popular for picnics and photography.

    Palolem Beach is a picturesque, crescent-shaped paradise in South Goa. Unlike the more crowded beaches of North Goa, Palolem offers a more peaceful and laid-back vibe, making it ideal for travelers seeking tranquility. The clear blue waters, surrounded by green hills, make it perfect for swimming, kayaking, and enjoying water sports. Visitors can unwind in hammocks or relax on the beach while enjoying the serene surroundings. Palolem also offers a range of beachfront cottages and boutique hotels, making it an ideal getaway for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

    Elephant Junction

    Elephant Junction:A unique experience where visitors can interact with elephants, enjoy elephant rides, and witness cultural performances.

    Chapora Fort, perched on a hilltop overlooking the Vagator Beach, is famous for its scenic views and cinematic history. The fort gained popularity after being featured in the Bollywood movie "Dil Chahta Hai," where it became the backdrop for iconic scenes. Though the fort itself is now in ruins, its surroundings offer one of the most stunning panoramic views of the Arabian Sea, the Chapora River, and the neighboring beaches. It’s a great spot for photography, picnics, and exploring the remnants of the fort while enjoying the cool breeze and breathtaking landscape.
